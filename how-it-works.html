Payments-aware workflow, not generic automation

How Fincline rebuilds payment context before your team has to chase it

Fincline connects your bank, accounting system, and inbox so reconciliation starts with the full picture, not just a bank line item with missing details.

Connect once

Read-only connections bring together the systems your team already uses.

Reconstruct context

Payments, invoices, remittance signals, and history are evaluated together.

Review only exceptions

Your team spends less time on detective work and more time on decisions.

Step 1
1

Connect the systems that already hold the clues

Fincline is useful because it sits across the fragmented sources your team currently checks one by one.

🏦

Bank connection

Fincline connects to your business bank account with read-only access so it can ingest cleared transactions and settlement activity without touching funds.

📘

QuickBooks or Xero

Open invoices, customer records, vendor data, and accounting context are pulled in so the matching process starts from what is expected, not from scratch.

✉️

Email inbox

Fincline reads invoice attachments and remittance emails so it can use real payment references and supporting evidence that usually live outside the ledger.

🔗

One connected workflow

Once these sources are connected, Fincline can evaluate transactions in context instead of forcing your team to jump between bank feeds, accounting records, and email.

Step 2
2

Fincline reconstructs what actually happened

This is where the product becomes payments-aware instead of just rule-based.

⚡

Transaction arrives

A cleared ACH, wire, card settlement, or other inbound payment is ingested from the bank connection and normalized into a consistent transaction record.

🧠

Context is assembled

Fincline compares amount, payer clues, invoice data, remittance references, timing, and historical patterns to determine what the payment most likely represents.

📉

Net-of-fees and partials are handled correctly

Instead of treating all differences as mismatches, Fincline recognizes settlement deductions, partial payments, split payments, and installment patterns as first-class cases.

📬

Remittance data strengthens the match

If a remittance email arrives before the cash clears, Fincline can use that evidence to narrow candidates and make the final bank-side match more explainable.

🔎 Fincline is designed to explain why a match is suggested. It is not meant to feel like a black box making unexplained bookkeeping decisions.
Step 3
3

The right action is routed based on confidence

Routine items should move quickly. Edge cases should surface with context.

✅

High-confidence items move forward

When the context is strong enough, the transaction can be treated as routine and moved through the workflow with a clear audit trail.

👆

Mid-confidence items go to quick review

Users see the suggested match, the reasoning behind it, and the likely alternatives so they can confirm or reassign without hunting for clues manually.

🚩

Low-confidence items are surfaced, not forced

If Fincline is not confident, it does not pretend otherwise. The item is sent to review with the most useful context available for investigation.

🧾

Every decision stays explainable

Recommended matches, confirmations, overrides, and exceptions all remain visible in an audit-friendly workflow designed for operational trust.

Why This Matters

Fincline reduces manual context recovery

The biggest time sink in reconciliation is usually not clicking confirm. It is reconstructing what happened across multiple systems before anyone can decide.

Fewer unknown payers

Transactions arrive with better supporting context, reducing the time spent asking who sent this and what it belongs to.

Faster morning review

Instead of reviewing everything, your team reviews the smaller set of items that genuinely need judgment.

More trust in the workflow

Because Fincline shows reasoning and alternatives, users can act with more confidence than they would with unexplained automation.
