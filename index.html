The Problem

Why closing the books still takes too long

The hardest part is not posting the entry. It is figuring out what a payment means before the entry can be made. That detective work slows close, creates uncertainty, and burns hours every cycle.

1

Payments arrive without enough context

ACH, wires, payouts, and settlements hit the bank with weak references, vague memos, or no usable invoice detail at all.

2

Teams manually reconstruct what happened

Bookkeepers bounce across the bank, accounting system, inbox, and spreadsheets to understand who paid, what was covered, and what is still open.

3

Exceptions pile up near close

Partials, split payments, net-of-fees deposits, and grouped settlements sit unresolved until they become month-end blockers.

How It Works

Fincline gives reconciliation the missing context

Instead of forcing your team to guess from a raw bank line item, Fincline combines bank activity, invoice data, remittance signals, and inbox context into one review workflow.

1

See all incoming payment activity

Connect your bank, accounting system, and inbox with read-only access so Fincline can see the same fragmented signals your team currently pieces together by hand.

2

Match payments with reasoning

Fincline classifies payment behavior, suggests likely matches, and explains why a payment belongs to an invoice, a group of invoices, or a review queue.

3

Confirm faster and close earlier

High-confidence items move forward quickly. Only uncertain items go to review, so your team spends time on exceptions instead of routine work.

Why Fincline

Built for real-world payment complexity

Fincline is designed for the messy reality of finance operations where payer names vary, amounts arrive net of fees, and one payment does not always equal one invoice.

Payment-aware matching

Handles ACH, wires, card payouts, BNPL settlements, partial payments, split payments, and grouped deposits without treating everything unusual as an exception.

Explainable confidence

Every suggested match comes with reasoning, confidence, and rule context so the user understands the recommendation before acting on it.

Exception-first workflow

Routine work is surfaced for fast confirmation. Hard items are separated into review and investigation queues with the right context attached.

Custom rules for your business

Apply organization-specific logic for recurring vendor behavior, fee tolerances, payer aliases, or invoice patterns so the workflow matches how your team actually works.

Read-only until confirmation

Fincline is designed for trust. It analyzes and explains first, then writes back only after explicit user action.

Audit-friendly by design

Matches, rules, confirmations, and exceptions are visible and reviewable so teams can move faster without losing control.
